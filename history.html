Early History

  • Paleolithic Age (about 2 million years ago)
    • Archeologists date the region of Jordan to go back into these ages and the appearance of stone tools support this.
    • Jordanians at this point were hunter-gathers before they became settlers later on.
  • Neolithic Age (10,000 B.C.E.)
    • Settled communities became more common in the region.
    • Agriculture and trade became a regular practice during this time.
    • Large stone monuments called dolmens were crafted to resemble people. It is not known as of why these monuments were created but some say that they might represent the dead due to their human resemblance.
    • Sites containing dolmens were very religious and were visited for worship and other holy endeavors.
    • Pottery started to become a practice which increased the wealth of many, while also circulating through trade for its value and aesthetics.

Bronze Age (3000 - 1200 B.C.E.)

  • Further improvements in ceramics, agriculture, and architecture.
  • Khirbet Iskander, a Bronze Age settlement founded in 2350 B.C.E., was a well-known trading spot until it was invaded later in 2100 B.C.E.
    • These invaders destroyed towns, villages, and cities within Jordan.
  • Another group, called The Hyksos, took down these invaders and conquered Egypt in around 2000 B.C.E.
    • The Hyksos made a significant impact on Jordan be changing the culture entirely, while also introducing the chariot, horses, a composite bow, improved way to funnel water through the land, and new ideas for improving the city’s surrounding defense.
  • The Hyksos were driven out in 1570 B.C.E. by the Egyptians who made the land.
    • Writing took into development after the Egyptians took control.

Iron Age (1200 - 330 B.C.E.)

  • The Sea People arrived in Canaan in 1200 B.C.E.
    • They had an experienced skill set in the extraction and use of metals for weapons and tools.
  • The Mesha Stele, an ancient artifact, is one of the best-known artifacts that told the story of the battle fought between Meshas, the king of Moab, and the three kings of Israel.
    • Many have noticed great value within the stone artifact but there are many others who see it as just another tablet of ancient texts.
  • The Sea People were claimed to be defeated by Rameses III in 1178 B.C.E., with the Assyrian Empire taking control soon after.

The Reign of Powerful Kingdoms

  • The Assyrian (Neo-Assyrian) Empire, with its knowledge of metallurgy and political power, maintained control of the empire until it’s crash in 612 B.C.E.
  • The Babylonian Empire took control from 612 – 549 B.C.E., with Cyrus the Great, leader of the Achaemenid Empire, taking it from the Babylon’s and keeping control from 549 - 330 B.C.E.
  • Alexander the Great seized the land from Cyrus in 331 B.C.E.
    • With Alexander came the rise of the Nabateans and the famed pink city of Petra.
  • The Nabateans were nomads who obtained their wealth through trade and carved the city of Petra into what it’s known today as “The Treasury”.
  • The Nabateans had tremendous wealth and created a huge impact on the culture in Jordan: art, agriculture, trade, etc.
      They also made vast improvements to the structuring of wells, dams, and aqueducts (passageways for bodies of water to flow through).
  • The reign of the Nabatean kingdom lasted from 168 B.C.E. - 106 C.E., even though they were a part of the region for much longer

Shift to Modern Day

  • Rome took control of the region at around 64 - 63 B.C.E. and allowed the Nabateans to maintain under their control in the meantime
  • The Romans made major developments within the region by:
    • Creating a powerful trade center
    • Building roads, aqueducts, and temples
    • Revamping large areas into more fertile land
  • Rome fell to the Byzantine Empire in 476 C.E.
  • Jordan became a part of the Umayyad Empire, an empire that embodied the Muslim religion and ruled from 661 – 750 C.E.
    • The region was soon forgotten by the next ruling empire, the Abassids, that ruled from 750 – 1258 C.E.
    • This also spurred conflict with the Byzantine Empire that was still trying to keep hold of power in Jordan
  • The Ottoman Empire defeated the Byzantine Empire in 1453 C.E. and kept control of the region until World War I where they weakened and collapsed in 1923 C.E.
  • Jordan became a part of the British Empire but regained their independence after World War II in 1946 C.E.
  • The kingdom is now known as The Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan and thrive economically, aesthetically, and independently
