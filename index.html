Intro To Jordan

When the individual thinks of a sandy region, dry lands, and an abundance of blocky tanned buildings, their first thought may be Egypt, Iran, Syria, or even Yemen. What people don’t know is that there is another sandy king in town that goes by the name of Jordan. With a population of about 11.2 million, this Hashemite Kingdom resides in the middle east of southern Asia alongside the Jordan river. The land of Jordan, consisting of mostly desert, is full of Arabs who speak their native Arabic language and practice their religion of Islam.


The capital and largest city within this region is the city of Amman. Another well-known city from Jordan goes by the name of Petra. A traveler, Gertrude Bell, described it as a “fairy tale city” and “ pink and wonderful” due to the city’s vibrant warm colors that permeate throughout it, making for a great tourist spot for travelers.
Although Jordan may seem like the perfect city to visit, it does have its fair share of history. Since B.C.E., Jordan has struggled to get ahold of its land due to constant invading from nearby kingdoms and political issues.
Jordan was only able to gain independence of its country in 1949 and further signed a peace treaty with the Israel in 1994, granting Jordan the most freedom than it has ever had throughout history. Thus, in the present, Jordan claims itself as a visually aesthetic region that strives economically and holds much of its history through archaeological pieces.
